# Vision

## Building the Future of Game Server Operations

Deadbelt Operations Platform (DOP) exists to redefine how game server infrastructure is managed.

Today's server administrators often rely on numerous disconnected tools to deploy servers, manage configurations, synchronize modifications, monitor health, automate maintenance, coordinate communities, and maintain operational visibility. While these tools solve individual problems well, they rarely function as a unified platform.

DOP was created to change that.

---

# Our Vision

Our vision is to build the leading open-source operations platform for dedicated game server infrastructure.

Rather than replacing existing ecosystems, DOP aims to unify them through a modular, extensible platform that enables administrators to manage every aspect of their environments from a single, cohesive interface.

The platform should empower communities—not lock them into proprietary ecosystems.

---

# What We Believe

We believe that:

* Open-source software drives innovation through collaboration.
* Automation should reduce repetitive work, not reduce administrator control.
* Infrastructure should be defined by desired state rather than manual configuration.
* Integrations should rely on documented interfaces and open standards whenever possible.
* Communities should own their infrastructure and remain free to choose the services that best fit their needs.

---

# Looking Beyond Today

The initial implementation of DOP focuses on DayZ because it presents a complex operational environment with numerous opportunities for automation and orchestration.

However, DOP is intentionally designed as a platform rather than a single-purpose application.

As the project matures, the same architecture should support additional game server ecosystems, deployment models, hosting providers, and operational workflows through modular extensions.

---

# Long-Term Objectives

Over time, DOP aims to provide:

* Unified environment management
* Intelligent deployment automation
* Extension-based provider integrations
* Infrastructure monitoring and observability
* Configuration synchronization
* Automated backup and recovery
* Community management integrations
* Secure remote administration
* Comprehensive APIs and SDKs
* Cross-platform operation

Future research may include artificial intelligence and machine learning capabilities that assist administrators with operational insights, anomaly detection, predictive maintenance, and intelligent automation while preserving human oversight and decision-making.

---

# Community First

DOP is intended to be more than software.

It is intended to become a collaborative platform where developers, server administrators, content creators, hosting providers, and community managers work together to improve the operational experience for everyone.

Core platform functionality will remain free and open source under the Apache License 2.0, ensuring that communities retain ownership of their infrastructure and their future.

---

# Looking Ahead

Success will not be measured solely by the number of servers managed by DOP.

Success will be measured by the strength of the community that builds, extends, documents, and improves the platform together.

The goal is not simply to create another server management application.

The goal is to establish an open, extensible operations platform that serves as the foundation for the next generation of game server infrastructure management.
